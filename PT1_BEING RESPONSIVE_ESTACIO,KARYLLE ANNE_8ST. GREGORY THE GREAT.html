Widely regarded as one of the greatest point guards of all time and as the greatest shooter in NBA history Curry is credited with revolutionizing basketball by inspiring teams and players to routinely utilize the three-point shot.

2. Kobe Bryant

Kobe Bryant was an American professional basketball player. A shooting guard, he spent his entire 20-year career with the Los Angeles Lakers in the National Basketball Association (NBA). Widely regarded as one of the greatest basketball players of all time, Bryant also led the NBA in scoring twice, and ranks fourth in league all-time regular season and postseason scoring. He was posthumously voted into the Naismith Memorial Basketball Hall of Fame in 2020.

Born:August 23, 1978 Philadelphia, Pennsylvania

Died:January 26, 2020 (aged 41)Calabasas, California

Height:6 ft 6 in (1.98 m)

Why them?

When it came to basketball players, they were my first choice. I know there are many other good basketball players, but I will pick them over and over again because they were a legend and a hero to me. I've been watching them since I was a kid, and they're also the reason I enjoy playing basketball. They inspire a lot of people, including myself. I'm actually sure how depressed I was when Kobe died in 2020, but I'm fine now. The three of them taught me that life isn't always about winning; sometimes we have to accept defeat and go on.
